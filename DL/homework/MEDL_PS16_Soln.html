Problem 1. Rocket Launch (Tensorflow)¶

  • Rocket was launched from the ground but the rocket's fuel injector malfunctioned.
  • Fuel is now injected periodically, so the rocket has periodic vibration on its vertical velocity
  • Rocket scientists computed the equation for the vertical velocity and the equation is given below.


$$\frac{du}{dt} = 0.12 e^{0.02t} - 0.12 \cos({0.2 \pi t})$$

  • Now, we want to predict the vertical displacement of the rocket when given the time after the launch

(3) Define the ODE system we will use in this problem

  • Hint: the rocket was launched from the ground (consider this condition as the initial condition)
  • $u$ is the vertical displacement and $\frac{du}{dt}$ is the vertical velocity
  • Multiply your IC_loss with 0.01
In [4]:
def ode_system(t, net):

    t = t.reshape(-1, 1)
    t = np.vstack([t, [0]])
    t = tf.constant(t, dtype = tf.float32)    
    t_0 = tf.zeros((1, 1))
    one = tf.zeros((1, 1))   
    
    with tf.GradientTape() as tape:
        tape.watch(t)         
        u = net(t)
        u_t = tape.gradient(u, t)
    
    ode_loss = u_t - (0.12 * tf.math.exp(0.02 * t) - 0.12 * tf.math.cos(0.2 * np.pi * t))
    IC_loss = 0.01 * (net(t_0) - one)
    
    square_loss = tf.square(ode_loss) + tf.square(IC_loss)
    total_loss = tf.reduce_mean(square_loss)
    
    return total_loss

Problem 2. Burger's Equation (DeepXDE)¶

  • Burger's Equation is a fundamental partial differential equation and convection–diffusion equation occurring in various areas of applied mathematics, such as fluid mechanics, nonlinear acoustics, gas dynamics, and traffic flow.

  • For a given field ${\displaystyle u(x,t)}$ and diffusion coefficient (or kinematic viscosity, as in the original fluid mechanical context) ${\displaystyle \nu }$ , the general form of Burgers' equation (also known as viscous Burgers' equation) in one space dimension is the dissipative system:


$$ \begin{align*} {\partial u \over \partial t} + u{\partial u \over \partial x} = \nu {\partial^2 u \over \partial x^2} \end{align*} $$

  • Dirichlet Boundary Conditions: ${\displaystyle u(-1,t) = u(1,t) = 0}$
  • Initial Conditions: ${\displaystyle u(x,0) = -\sin (\pi x)}$

def pde(x, y):
    dy_x = dde.grad.jacobian(y, x, i = 0, j = 0)
    dy_t = dde.grad.jacobian(y, x, i = 0, j = 1)
    dy_xx = dde.grad.hessian(y, x, i = 0, j = 0)
    return dy_t + y * dy_x - 0.01 / np.pi * dy_xx
