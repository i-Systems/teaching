Problem 3: GCN

As you know, MLP uses the features of the given data to predict the class of each data. However, it cannot effectively model the conectivity among data. Therefore, in this problem we will compare the performance of MLP and GCN for a dataset with a graph stucture.

Given dataset is the Pubmed citation data. PubMed is a free search engine accessing primarily the MEDLINE database of references and abstracts on life sciences and biomedical topics. Basically, Pubmed data is very similar to the 'Cora' dataset that we have practiced during the class. Visit https://graphneural.network/datasets/ and https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PubMed to fully understand the given data.

  • The Pubmed dataset consists of 19717 nodes classified into one of three classes.

  • The citation network consists of 108365 links.

  • Each publication in the dataset is described by a TF/IDF weighted word vector indicating the absence/presence of the corresponding word from the dictionary which consists of 500 unique words.
